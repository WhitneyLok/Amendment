Key dates:

  • 1866: Congress decided to pass this amendment.
  • 1868: It was ratified to extend the idea of liberties and rights to include former slaves.
  • 1896: Plessy vs Ferguson.
  • 1954: Brown vs Board of Education of Topeka.


Key figures and facts:

  • Plessy vs Ferguson- It was the first landmark case to actually test the 14th amendment. The supreme court deemed that segregation was constitutionally acceptable as long as the colored and white areas were equal. This “separate but equal” meant that from the water fountain to schools were all segregated because it was legal and does not violate the constitution.






  • Brown vs Board of Education: it was important because after 60 more years after the case of Plessy vs Ferguson the Supreme court decides to take another look. After another look, the supreme court deemed that “separate educational facilities are inherently unequal” Thus supreme court ruled that separate schools violated the 14th amendment and this began the desegregation.









  • Random fact: During June of 2015, the 14th amendment was used in the supreme court to legalize gay marriage across 50 states. This was achieved by using the equal protection of the 14th amendment and that denial of gay same-sex marriage is “unjustified infringement of the fundamental right to marry”
